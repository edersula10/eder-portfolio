Local SEO: The 4 Things That Actually Matter

Forget 50-point checklists. These are the only things moving the needle for local small businesses looking to get found on Google.

Every week I see small business owners getting sold on expensive SEO packages full of technical jargon and vague promises. Keyword density reports. Backlink audits. Schema markup reviews. Most of it is noise.

For a local small business in the GTA — a contractor in Vaughan, a salon in North York, a restaurant in Woodbridge — local SEO comes down to four things. Do these four things well and you will show up when the people around you are looking for exactly what you offer.

"Near me" searches have grown over 500% in recent years. People are looking for businesses like yours in real time, on their phones, ready to spend money. The question is whether they can find you.

01

Google Business Profile — Fully Completed

This is the single most impactful thing you can do for local SEO, and it's completely free. Your Google Business Profile is what shows up in the map pack when someone searches "plumber near me" or "hair salon Etobicoke." If your profile is incomplete, outdated, or missing — you're invisible in those results. Fill in every field: business hours, services, photos, phone number, website URL. Add real photos of your work, your space, and your team. Google rewards complete profiles with higher placement. This takes an afternoon and it costs nothing.

02

Google Reviews — Consistently Getting Them

Reviews are the most powerful local ranking signal after your Business Profile itself. Not just for rankings — but for conversions. When someone finds your business and sees 47 five-star reviews, they call. When they see 3 reviews from 2021, they keep scrolling. The key word is consistently. Five reviews a month beats fifty reviews in January and none since. After every job, every service, every satisfied customer — ask for a review. Send them the direct link. Make it frictionless. Build a system around it. Your competitors aren't doing this, which means it's your fastest path to standing out.

03

Your Website — With Location-Specific Content

Google needs to understand where you are and who you serve. Your website should clearly mention the cities, neighbourhoods, and regions you work in — not buried in the footer, but naturally throughout your content. A contractor serving the GTA should have their homepage mention Vaughan, Woodbridge, Mississauga, North York. A salon in Etobicoke should say "Etobicoke" on their page, not just "Toronto." This isn't keyword stuffing — it's giving Google the geographic context it needs to show your business to people in those areas. Your website and your Google Business Profile should tell the exact same story about who you are and where you operate.

04

Consistent NAP — Name, Address, Phone Number

NAP consistency is one of the most overlooked and most impactful local SEO factors. Google cross-references your business information across your website, Google Business Profile, Yelp, Facebook, Yellow Pages, and dozens of other directories. If your phone number is slightly different on Yelp than on your website, or your address is formatted differently across platforms — Google loses confidence in your listing and ranks you lower. Audit every place your business appears online and make sure the name, address, and phone number are identical everywhere. Exactly the same. Every time.

What About Everything Else?

Backlinks, meta descriptions, schema markup, keyword research tools — these things matter at a certain scale and for certain types of businesses. But for a local service business with under 50 employees trying to rank in their city or neighbourhood? They are not where your time and money should go first.

Get your Google Business Profile filled out completely. Get your customers leaving reviews consistently. Make sure your website mentions where you actually work. Make sure your business info is the same everywhere online. Do those four things well and you will see real results — more calls, more form submissions, more customers walking through your door.

Local SEO isn't complicated. It just requires consistency. Start today, not next month.
