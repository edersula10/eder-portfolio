Every extra second your site takes to load costs you customers. Here's the simple fix most businesses are missing entirely.

What Actually Slows Websites Down

Most slow small business websites have the same problems. They're not complicated to fix, but you need to know what to look for.

  • Unoptimized images — A photo taken on an iPhone is 4–8MB. A website image should be under 200KB. That single difference can make your site 5x faster.
  • Too many plugins — WordPress sites with 20+ plugins are loading 20+ extra files on every page visit. Most of those plugins are doing almost nothing.
  • Cheap shared hosting — If your site is on a $3/month hosting plan, your server is slow. Period. Hosting is not where you cut corners.
  • No caching — Without caching, your server rebuilds every page from scratch every single time someone visits. With caching, it serves a pre-built version in milliseconds.
  • Blocking fonts and scripts — Third-party fonts and JavaScript files that load before your page content will delay everything else from appearing.

The Real Cost of a Slow Website

Let's make this concrete. Say your website gets 500 visitors a month from Google and social media. If your site loads in 5 seconds, roughly half of those visitors leave before they even see your business. That's 250 potential customers who never got the chance to contact you.

Even if only 10% of those people would have called you, that's 25 missed inquiries every month. For a trades company charging $500–$2,000 per job, a slow website could be costing you thousands of dollars monthly — and you'd never even know.

Google officially uses page speed as a ranking factor. A faster website doesn't just keep visitors — it brings in more visitors in the first place.

How to Check Your Website Speed Right Now

Go to pagespeed.web.dev and enter your website URL. Google will give you a score from 0–100 and a breakdown of exactly what's slowing your site down. Aim for 90+ on mobile. Anything under 70 is a problem that's actively costing you leads.

Pay attention to the "Largest Contentful Paint" metric — that's how long it takes for the main content of your page to appear. This should be under 2.5 seconds. If it's over 4 seconds, visitors are gone before they've read a single word.

The Fix Is Simpler Than You Think

For most small business websites, speed problems come down to three things: image sizes, hosting quality, and how the site was built. A modern, well-coded website built on clean HTML and CSS — without a bloated page builder or dozens of plugins — will load fast by default.
